Overview

The Islamic Republic of Pakistan is a country in South Asia, founded on August 14, 1947. Named after the concept "Pak-stan" (land of the pure), it was created as a homeland for Muslims of the Indian subcontinent. Pakistan is the world's fifth-most populous country and the second-largest Muslim-majority nation.

Geography

Mountain Ranges

  • Karakoram Range: Includes K2, the second-highest mountain in the world (8,611 m)
  • Hindu Kush: Major mountain range in the north
  • Himalayas: Northeast portion with peaks over 7,000 meters
  • Sulaiman Range: Western mountain system

Major Rivers

  • Indus River: Main river, 2,900 km long, crucial for irrigation and economy
  • Sutlej, Chenab, Ravi, Beas: Tributaries in Punjab
  • Baloch and Makran: Coastal regions

Climate Zones

  • Alpine and tundra in the north
  • Temperate in mountainous regions
  • Arid and semi-arid in the south and interior
  • Tropical in coastal areas
