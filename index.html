History of Pakistan

From ancient empires to independence and beyond

2300 - 1300 BCE

Indus Valley Civilization

One of the world's earliest urban civilizations flourished in the Indus Valley, with advanced city planning and trade networks.

1000 - 600 BCE

Vedic & Persian Periods

Rise of Vedic kingdoms and Persian dominion, with notable figures like Buddha and Mahavira during this transformative era.

500 - 323 BCE

Mauryan Empire

Under Emperor Ashoka, the Mauryan Empire brought unprecedented unity and prosperity to the region.

712 CE

Islamic Period Begins

Muhammad bin Qasim's conquest marked the beginning of Islamic influence and centuries of cultural synthesis.

1526 - 1857

Mughal Empire

The magnificent Mughal dynasty ruled for centuries, leaving an incredible architectural and cultural legacy.

1857 - 1947

British Colonial Period

British Raj transformed the region through infrastructure development while independence movements emerged.

14 August 1947

Independence

Pakistan emerged as an independent nation with Muhammad Ali Jinnah as the Father of the Nation.

  • Indus Valley - Fertile plains
  • Balochistan Plateau - Unique terrain
  • Thar Desert - Golden sands
  • Sindh & Punjab - Agricultural heartlands
  • Arabian Coast - Beautiful beaches
